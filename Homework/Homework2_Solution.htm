Instructions for assessment

1a) Finding a route between two locations in a city involves the road network, which corresponds to a discrete graph (5 points). Although it would be possible to plan on the continuous space that is given by the area covered with roads, this would be highly inefficient, so you would definitely want to use Dijkstra or A*. (5 points)

1b) As you are not only interested in distance, but also the number of turns, any linear combination of the two is a feasible cost function (5 points). Deduct three points for solutions that divide by the number of turns or subtract them, as this does the opposite than what was intended. 

2) The ratio will be A_free / A_total (10 points). Deduct points for the inverse or solutions involving constants (A_obstacles e.g.) that were not provided. The idea is that points are sampled from the entire area A_total, but only points from within A_free can be added to the tree. The likelihood to sample from free space is given by A_free/A_total, and this is also the ratio the number of points in A_free and A_total approaches for infinite number of samples. 

3) It takes O(log N) to search through a k-d tree as a tree to store N values will have log(N) depth. (10 points). Half the points for solutions like (N log N) or O(N). The worst case is not what was asked. 

4) As the signal has to travel to the obstacle and back, increasing the range (or equivalently the dynamic range in this case) will require the sensor to wait longer (5 points). This is not the case for a laser scanner where the limiting factor is not the time it takes the light to travel, but to sweep the laser. (5 points) 

5a) The signal has to travel a total of 30m, which takes 0.1s at 300m/s (10 points). Half the points for calculating only "one way". 

5b) It also takes 0.1s to hear back from the laser scanner. (5 points) Any calculations involving the speed of light are not relevant here as the bandwidth is provided in the question.

6a) The GPS has a precision of 3m (5 points) and an accuracy of 30m (5 points).

6b) 18000 readings per hour are 300 readings per minute, which is 5 readings per second. The bandwidth of the sensor is therefore 5 Hz. (10 points).
